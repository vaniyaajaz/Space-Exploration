03 HERE'S YOUR SPACECRAFTS

How do we even get to space?
Click below to find out exactly what it takes
to fly away on your next space mission

The Command Vehicle

Orion Spacecraft

The lifeboat for the crew. It’s a rugged capsule designed by NASA to sustain four
astronauts for 21 days in deep space. It features a heavy-duty heat shield and a European Service
Module for power and air.

The Heavy Lifter

Space Launch System

The "muscle." This is NASA’s massive rocket that provides 8.8 million pounds of
thrust. Its only job is to push the Orion capsule out of Earth’s gravity and onto a path toward the Moon.

Future Base Camp

The Lunar Getaway

The "outpost." A small international space station orbiting the Moon. It acts as a
permanent hub where astronauts can park their spacecraft, switch to a lander, or conduct long-term
science experiments.

The Landing Option

SpaceX Starship HLS

The "heavy lifter." A fully reusable, massive ship built by SpaceX for cargo and dozens
of passengers. NASA will use a specialized version to land humans on the lunar surface, while
SpaceX intends it for future Mars colonies.
